About

Abdelkarim
Laglil Herradi

PhD Researcher · Universidad Carlos III de Madrid

I am a PhD researcher in the Social Sciences at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid (UC3M), where my work sits at the intersection of political sociology, discourse analysis, and international political economy. My research focuses on migration and anti-immigration rhetorics and politics in Europe, examining how populist and nativist discourses are constructed, mobilized, and institutionalized across different national contexts.

As a secondary line of inquiry, I am cultivating a growing interest in the international political economy of remittances in the Global South — exploring how financial flows from migrant workers shape development trajectories, household strategies, and state-society relations.

Research

Primary Line

Migration & Anti-Immigration Politics in Europe

I investigate the discursive and political dynamics driving anti-immigration sentiment in Europe — from far-right party platforms to mainstream policy convergence, and the rhetorical frames that make exclusionary politics legible and appealing across different publics.

Secondary Line

IPE of Remittances in the Global South

This emerging line of research interrogates remittances not merely as economic transfers but as political and developmental phenomena — examining their relationship to dependency, state capacity, financialization, and transnational household governance in sending countries.
